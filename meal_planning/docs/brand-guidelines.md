# Section 02
# VERBAL IDENTITY

---

## How Palate Talks

Like a friend who's into food but not annoying about it.

Curious. Direct. Warm but not soft. We don't lecture. We don't guilt. We notice things and point them out. We get excited about variety the way some people get excited about wine or coffee or sneakers.

We're specific, not vague. "You're low on fermented foods" not "Consider diversifying your intake."

We're human, not corporate. "Your greens are looking lonely" not "Leafy vegetable consumption below optimal threshold."

---

## Voice Principles

**Be direct.**
Say it plainly. No jargon. No wellness-speak. If a 12-year-old wouldn't understand it, rewrite it.

**Be warm, not soft.**
We're encouraging, not permissive. We care enough to tell you when your eating is narrow. That's not mean. That's helpful.

**Be specific.**
"Legumes" not "plant-based proteins." "3 more plants to hit 30" not "You're doing great, keep going!"

**Be playful when it fits.**
A little wit goes a long way. But don't force it. Funny isn't the goal. Clear is the goal. Funny is a bonus.

---

## We Say

- "Your palette's looking a bit narrow this week"
- "Blind spot alert: no fermented foods in 2 weeks"
- "You're 3 plants away from 30. Almost there."
- "Nice range. You hit 8 colors this week."
- "Same meal again? Nothing wrong with thatâ€”but here's some inspiration if you want it."

## We Don't Say

- "Great job! You're doing amazing!" (empty praise)
- "Consider incorporating more diverse food groups" (corporate)
- "Nourish your body with rainbow goodness" (wellness fluff)
- "Optimize your nutritional intake" (robotic)
- "You need to eat more vegetables" (parental)

## Typography

**Headlines:** Something with warmth. Rounded but not childish. GT Walsheim, Circular, or similar.

**Body:** Clean and readable. Inter, DM Sans, whatever works at small sizes.

**Numbers/Data:** Slightly technical. Space Grotesk or similar. Scores should feel precise.

---

## Photography

**Style:** Overhead food shots. Natural light. Lots of color on the plate.

**The key:** Every photo should show variety. No single-ingredient shots. No sad solo salads. We want plates that look like someone's eating wide.

**Avoid:** Dark/moody food photography. Over-styled "food porn." Anything that looks like it belongs in a diet ad.

---
